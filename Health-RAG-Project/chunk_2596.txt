1 Introduction Background Noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) are the worlds leading cause of death, responsible for an estimated 41 million of the 55 million deaths in 2019 (1). Nearly half of these deaths were premature (i.e. in people aged less than 70 years) and occurred in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs).
