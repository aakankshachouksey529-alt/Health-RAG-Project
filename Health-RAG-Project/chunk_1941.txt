grandmothers are trained to counsel people for structured 45-minute sessions on wooden benches within the grounds of primary care clinics, where people access services for various medical conditions, including HIV/AIDS, TB, NCDs and NTDs. People are referred to the benches by medical care providers and, after the first session, are invited back by the grandmothers for up to five more sessions. They may also receive home visits, join group therapy or be referred to a specialist, depending on their needs.
