The cumulative mortality burden of mental health conditions can be derived using natural history models that relate prevalence to observed rates of excess deaths. These models are not part of the estimation of fatal burden (see section 3.1.3 Burden), which attributes deaths to the primary cause (such as cardiovascular disease), but researchers have used these models to show that the mortality burden of mental health conditions is grossly underestimated.
