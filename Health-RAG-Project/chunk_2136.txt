Researchers estimate the rates of non-affective psychosis and depression among these prisoners are 6.2% and 16.0% respectively, which is respectively 16 and 6 times higher than the rates among the general population (535). Adolescents in the juvenile justice system are similarly much more likely to experience mental health conditions than those in the general population, with an estimated 70% having at least one diagnosable mental health condition (536).
