From 2018, the programme started integrating its services within some primary care networks, as well as within specific disease programmes for TB, HIV, lung health and diabetes. By December 2019, the programme reported that it had screened more than 100 000 people for anxiety and depression and given free mental health counselling to more than 9 000 people.
