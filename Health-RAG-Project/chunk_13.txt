(breathing) system consists of the nose, trachea, lungs, and diaphragm. Its primary function is to supply the blood with oxygen. When a person inhales, air enters the windpipe through the nose. The windpipe goes into the chest where it divides into two and goes into the right and left lungs. The air breathed in contains oxygen and this passes into the blood. Carbon dioxide (unclean air) in the body passes into the lungs and is breathed out. A normal adult breathes about 16 times and a child, about 20 times
