In a few cases, where hospital beds have been reduced without investment in community-based alternatives, the quality and quantity of care has suffered badly, leading to highly adverse outcomes for the people concerned. For example, in 2017 at least 144 people died, including from starvation and neglect, after they were discharged from hospitals to underfunded and underskilled community care as part of a rushed and poorly planned programme to deinstitutionalize mental health care in Gauteng, South Africa
