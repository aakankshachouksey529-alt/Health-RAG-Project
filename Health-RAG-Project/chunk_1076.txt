The review also showed that some second-generation antipsychotics on the model list for schizophrenia (risperidone and clozapine) were only included in 35% and 25% of national essential medicines lists in low-income countries respectively. The actual availability of different essential psychotropic medicines was lower in the public sector compared with the private sector, and well below 50% worldwide, often much lower in low-income countries where there is a high incidence of stock-outs.
