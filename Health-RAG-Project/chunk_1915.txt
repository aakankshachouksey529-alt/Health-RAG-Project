In 2019, Italy had no beds in psychiatric hospitals, while in the other G7 countries the number of psychiatric hospital beds ranged from 11 per 100 000 population in Canada to 170 per 100 000 in Japan (5). In Italy there are also fewer beds for acute care in general hospitals but more beds in community residential facilities than in the other G7 countries.
