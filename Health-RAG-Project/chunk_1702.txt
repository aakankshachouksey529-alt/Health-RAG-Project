In 1994, a Presidential Commission was established to draft a national policy and action plan on suicide prevention, and to coordinate action across multiple government agencies, including agriculture. Further bans in 1998 (endosulfan) and 2008 (dimethoate, fenthion and paraquat) boosted the decline. In total Sri Lanka banned 36 highly hazardous pesticides.
