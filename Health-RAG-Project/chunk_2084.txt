CASE STUDY BOX 7.18 Kenya: USP-K peer support groups The Users and Survivors of Psychiatry in Kenya (USP-K) is a peer-led national membership organization that works to promote the rights of people with psychosocial disabilities. As part of its activities, USP-K runs community-based peer support groups where people with lived experience can come together to share their stories, give each other psychosocial support and discuss specific issues, for example, crisis response strategies or local livelihoods.
