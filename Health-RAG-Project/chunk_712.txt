People with schizophrenia or other severe mental health conditions die on average 10 to 20 years earlier than the general population, often of preventable physical diseases. Overall, the economic consequences of mental health conditions are enormous. Produc- tivity losses and other indirect costs to society often far outstrip health care costs.
