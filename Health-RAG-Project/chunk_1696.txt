Banning the sale and use of acutely toxic, highly hazardous pesticides can lead to fewer deaths by suicide. It is a low-cost intervention, which is highly costeffective in countries with a high burden of pesticide self-poisonings (222, 361). National bans have been linked to a drop in pesticide suicides in Bangladesh, Jordan, Republic of Korea, Sri Lanka
