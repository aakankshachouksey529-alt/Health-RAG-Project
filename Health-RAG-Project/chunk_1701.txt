BOX 6.5 Sri Lanka: banning pesticides to prevent suicide When Sri Lankas government drew up the Control of Pesticides Act in 1980, pesticide poisoning accounted for more than two-thirds of all suicides in the country. In 1984, the Office of the Registrar of Pesticides began using its powers under the new Act to ban highly hazardous pesticides, starting with two organophosphorus insecticides (parathion and methyl parathion).
