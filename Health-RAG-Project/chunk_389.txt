It starts suddenly with:  Profuse (very large quantities) watery diarrhoea.  Vomiting.  Dehydration as the body loses a large amount of fluids quickly.  Muscle cramps.  Inadequate passing of urine. If the person is not given adequate fluids immediately to replace the fluid loss, there will be:  Sudden and rapid fall in blood pressure with the affected person having cold, damp skin.
